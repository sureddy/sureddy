## Core thesis

AI capability is accelerating faster than institutional trust. The bottleneck is no longer whether machines can generate, summarize, or code. The bottleneck is whether organizations can let autonomous systems act inside real environments, under real constraints, with memory, assurance, and accountability that hold up under audit.

Self-reporting is not verification. A system that grades its own homework cannot earn institutional trust, no matter how capable the underlying model. The systems that win will be the ones that can produce defensible evidence of why they should be trusted, and the architecture that produces that evidence has to remain neutral. An incumbent cannot independently audit its own enforcement.
