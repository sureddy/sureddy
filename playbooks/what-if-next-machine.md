# The What If Next Machine

## Complete Methodology and Application Playbook



*Reference edition, corrected. This is the canonical specification of the What If Next Machine, consolidated for practitioner use. It supersedes the first reference edition. The quantitative spine in Part Four carries one methodological fix, one framing correction, and two added disciplines, recorded in the changelog at the end. Read it once end to end before running a File. Return to Part Four whenever a number needs checking against a worked example.*



## Part One: The model, the Machine, and the File

Three names, three jobs, and the discipline holds only if they are not collapsed into one.

**What If Next** is the model. It is a way of seeing a market. The claim underneath it is that the hidden signal in any industry is not the technology everyone is discussing. It is the constraint that technology is exposing, the one binding limitation the whole system is organizing around whether anyone has named it or not.

**The What If Next Machine**, the Machine, is the engine. It is the procedure that converts a trigger, a deal, a thesis, a provocative public claim, into a structured, falsifiable, numerically grounded view of where a market is headed and why.

**The What If File**, a File, is the output. It is a living dossier, not a one-time essay. The thesis at the top is written to hold across many cycles. The branch weights, the signal ledger, and the kill conditions move every cycle as evidence arrives. A File is maintained the way a position is maintained, not published the way an article is published.

The split matters in plain speech. "The What If Next view of enterprise AI" describes a way of seeing. "I ran the Aisera acquisition through the Machine" describes an act. "File No. 9 is due for its quarterly pass" describes an artifact under maintenance. Confusing the three produces sloppy thinking, usually in the direction of treating one computed weight as a permanent truth rather than the current state of a living estimate.


## Part Two: The thesis underneath the Machine

Most market analysis follows visible motion: funding rounds, product launches, executive claims, adoption curves, the language a category adopts. Visible motion shows that something is happening. It rarely explains why the system is under pressure in that particular place and not somewhere else.

A market does not reorganize because everything about it is weak. It reorganizes because one or two constraints dominate the system at a given time, in the Theory of Constraints sense. Relieve anything other than the binding constraint and the system looks better in the spot you touched while the whole barely moves.

**The eight-type constraint taxonomy.** Naming the type of constraint is how you answer when, not only where.

| Type | What binds | Relief mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Technical | A capability does not yet work at the needed quality | A capability ships at the needed quality |
| Economic | The unit cost does not clear the buyer's payback threshold | Unit cost crosses the payback threshold |
| Regulatory | A ruling, statute, or enforcement posture forecloses the move | A ruling, statute, or enforcement action lands |
| Behavioral | Trust has not accrued for the buyer to act | Trust accrues through repeated safe use |
| Financial | The buyer cannot fund the change inside the current budget or balance sheet | Financing structure changes, or the ask shrinks |
| Operational | The surrounding process cannot absorb the capability without re-engineering | The process gets re-engineered around the capability |
| Cultural | The organization's identity or incentives resist the change regardless of logic | Incentives realign, or a champion forces the issue |
| Architectural | A required layer or standard does not yet exist for the capability to plug into | The missing layer or standard gets built |

**The type-to-clock table.** Where without when is the difference between being early and being right.

| Constraint type | Clock | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Technical | Engineering clock | Fast once feasible |
| Economic | Cost-curve clock | Predictable, watch the curve |
| Regulatory | Litigation and rulemaking clock | Slow and lumpy |
| Behavioral | Adoption-curve clock | Gradual, then sudden |
| Financial | Budget-cycle clock | Stepwise, tied to fiscal calendars |
| Operational | Process-redesign clock | Slow, organization-dependent |
| Cultural | Incentive-realignment clock | Slowest, resists forcing |
| Architectural | Standards-emergence clock | Lumpy, then fast once a standard tips |

Constraints blend. A File should name the dominant type and note any secondary type with its own clock, the way an antitrust posture sits as a regulatory constraint riding on top of an otherwise economic one. The blend, not just the primary type, is what determines the realistic timeline.



## Part Three: Detection, ownership, and the moat

### 3.1 The six tells (symptom-driven)

Read the market for symptoms of a binding constraint, independent of any structural model.

**Spend without return.** Money goes in and measurable ROI does not come out. The capital is paying to push against a wall.

**Where pilots die.** Initiatives reach proof of concept and stall at the same production gate. The thing that kills them at that point is the constraint.

**The universal workaround.** Everyone builds the same patch for the same gap. The patch marks the missing layer.

**The preemptive apology.** Every vendor apologizes for the same limitation before being asked. They are all bounded by the same wall.

**The not-yet chorus.** The people closest to the work keep saying the capability is not ready for the real thing. Listen for what specifically is not yet.

**Convergence.** Competitors independently reposition onto the same layer and start using the same word for it. That convergence names the constraint.

### 3.2 The force scan (structure-driven)

Run Porter's five forces plus complementors as a six-location coverage scan: buyer power, supplier power, new entrants, substitutes, rivalry, and complements. The sixth force, complementors, is the value-net extension to Porter's original five, and it is where AI-era constraints disproportionately hide, since a complementor's pricing or access decision can bind a market Porter's original five would read as healthy.

The discipline between the two methods: Porter lists, the Machine ranks. The force scan guarantees coverage so nothing is missed. The tells and the constraint logic decide which location is binding right now. A still Porter map shows which force holds power. The cascade, in Part Three Section 3.5, is that map in motion.

### 3.3 Ownership map and the owner's game

List who owns each layer of the value chain, then mark the layer that is binding. Watch for the case where no vendor owns it at all, where the binding constraint sits inside the buyer's own risk function or balance sheet rather than with any seller. When that happens, the prize goes to whoever relieves the buyer, not to whoever holds a layer.

Owners of a binding constraint are not static. Whoever owns it has every incentive to keep it binding and extract rent from it. Four owner moves, read against the buyer-side and challenger-side responses that erode them:

| Owner move | What it does | What erodes it |
|---|---|---|
| Defend | Hardens the wall, raises switching cost | A substitute routes around the wall entirely |
| Toll | Charges rent for passage without removing the wall | Regulation caps the toll, or a free alternative undercuts it |
| Absorb | Acquires the source of relief before it scales | The acquired capability is reproduced independently |
| Commoditize | A platform tears the wall down to win the layer above it | Whoever commoditizes captures the next layer up |

Naming the rent route names how the owner gets paid, and therefore what the owner will fight hardest to keep. A full ownership map states, for each layer, who pays, who captures, and who is liable if the layer fails.

### 3.4 The six moat tests

Run all six against any moat under examination. A moat is not binary; it is a profile across these six.

| Test | Question |
|---|---|
| Constraint fit | Does the moat sit on the binding constraint, or on a feature nobody is paying for? |
| Transferability | Does the advantage transfer to adjacent decisions, or only inside one narrow workflow? |
| Economic conversion | Has the moat actually converted to revenue, margin, or share, or is it still a story? |
| Control point leverage | Does owning this point of the system give leverage over the rest, or is it isolated? |
| Adversarial response | What does a well-funded rival do in response, and how long does the moat survive that response? |
| Learning compounding | Does the system get stronger with every deployment, customer, action, exception, and feedback loop? |

For a fast read under time pressure, score a moat on the two tests that matter most for its specific position: the one it passes most decisively, and the one that can kill it. That pairing is usually enough to place it.

### 3.5 The cascade

Relieving the binding constraint does not end the story. It relocates the constraint. The cascade traces that chain forward two or three links, ideally as a structural causal model with named paths, so the File anticipates where the fight moves next rather than declaring victory at the first resolution. Each link in the cascade is a market reorganization, and each link moves the control point. Map three links and the shape of the next several years becomes visible rather than just the next quarter.


## Part Four: The quantitative spine, in full

This part is the one most often named in prose and not actually run. A File that says "branch weights reflect a Bayesian update" without showing the likelihood vectors, the tempering, and the worked posterior has not run the spine. It has gestured at it. What follows is the complete mechanics, computed on a clean didactic example at every step so the arithmetic is checkable rather than asserted.

### 4.1 The belief layer, correctly named

Branch weights are the output of a **tempered, or power-likelihood, Bayesian update over a latent categorical hypothesis**. This is the precise name, and it matters because an earlier edition of this document called it a Dirichlet-multinomial conjugate update and justified it by conjugacy. That justification is false for the within-cycle step. A true Dirichlet-multinomial update observes outcome counts and adds them directly to the pseudo-count vector. What a File actually does within a cycle is multiply tempered likelihoods from signals bearing on a latent winning pattern, which produces a single point on the simplex, not a Dirichlet distribution over it.

The Dirichlet still does real work, but at a different step. It supplies the prior mean and prior strength going into the cycle, and it supplies genuine conjugate carryover at the cross-cycle count step described in Section 4.9. Inside a single cycle, the procedure is a robust-Bayes device: temper each signal's likelihood by an exponent set from its source grade, multiply across signals, multiply by the prior, and normalize. The arithmetic in this document is unchanged from any prior edition. Only the label and the justifying sentence needed correcting.

### 4.2 Redundancy and the conditional-independence assumption

The product-over-signals form in Section 4.1 assumes the signals are independent given the branch. State that assumption out loud, because the redundancy rule below is an approximate patch for the case where it fails, and a reader should be able to check whether a given ledger honors it.

When two signals report the same underlying fact through two outlets, a wire story and a press release describing the identical announcement, the second signal is tempered one additional step down from its grade-implied exponent. Without this correction, one fact dressed in two sources is counted as two independent pieces of evidence and inflates the posterior toward whatever that fact supports.

**Worked example.** A two-branch case, Branch X against Branch Y, flat 50-50 prior. Two Grade B signals, each with τ = 0.6, report the same underlying fact and both favor X: likelihood 0.75 for X, 0.388 for Y.

Counted naively, both signals at τ = 0.6:

- Tempered likelihood for X: 0.75^0.6 = 0.8415, applied twice, evidence weight = 0.8415² = 0.7081
- Tempered likelihood for Y: 0.388^0.6 = 0.5667, applied twice, evidence weight = 0.5667² = 0.3212
- Posterior on X: 0.7081 / (0.7081 + 0.3212) = 68.8%

Applying the redundancy correction, tempering the second signal one step down to τ = 0.3:

- First signal at τ = 0.6: X = 0.8415, Y = 0.5667
- Second signal at τ = 0.3: X = 0.75^0.3 = 0.9173, Y = 0.388^0.3 = 0.7529
- Evidence weight X = 0.8415 × 0.9173 = 0.7719; evidence weight Y = 0.5667 × 0.7529 = 0.4268
- Posterior on X: 0.7719 / (0.7719 + 0.4268) = 64.4%

The correction recovers 4.4 points of avoided double-counting. That looks small in one pair. Across a signal ledger with several correlated pairs, the cumulative correction can move a leading branch by three to four points, enough to change which branch leads.

### 4.3 The signal ledger and the likelihood vector, worked end to end

Each signal in the ledger carries an identifier, a one-line description, the source, the grade, the temper exponent, and a likelihood for every branch on a relative zero-to-one scale, representing how consistent that evidence is with that branch being the dominant pattern. Only the ratios across branches carry information, since the update normalizes at the end, so the scale is a convenience rather than a measured probability. These are analyst judgments, made explicit and held open to challenge, not measured probabilities. The ledger's value is in writing them down where a reader can dispute a single number rather than leaving them implicit in a paragraph.

**Worked example, the full ledger.** Three branches: Incumbent Absorbs, Insurgent Wins Category, and Substrate Commoditizes (the null, meaning the contested layer becomes a shared utility before either side wins it outright).

Prior, pseudo-counts α₀ = 10:

| Branch | α | Prior mean |
|---|---|---|
| Incumbent Absorbs | 4.0 | 40.0% |
| Insurgent Wins Category | 3.0 | 30.0% |
| Substrate Commoditizes (null) | 3.0 | 30.0% |

Signal ledger:

| ID | Signal | Grade | τ | Incumbent Absorbs | Insurgent Wins | Substrate Commoditizes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| S1 | Incumbent posts a realized revenue line tied to the contested feature | A | 1.0 | 0.80 | 0.30 | 0.25 |
| S2 | Vendor-adjacent analyst note on the insurgent's share gain | B | 0.6 | 0.35 | 0.75 | 0.35 |
| S3 | Founder interview projecting category capture | C | 0.2 | 0.30 | 0.70 | 0.30 |

Tempering (raw likelihood raised to τ):

| Signal | Incumbent Absorbs | Insurgent Wins | Substrate Commoditizes |
|---|---|---|---|
| S1 (τ=1.0) | 0.8000 | 0.3000 | 0.2500 |
| S2 (τ=0.6) | 0.5327 | 0.8415 | 0.5327 |
| S3 (τ=0.2) | 0.7860 | 0.9311 | 0.7860 |

Evidence weight, the product across signals:

- Incumbent Absorbs: 0.8000 × 0.5327 × 0.7860 = 0.33493
- Insurgent Wins Category: 0.3000 × 0.8415 × 0.9311 = 0.23506
- Substrate Commoditizes: 0.2500 × 0.5327 × 0.7860 = 0.10467

Posterior, prior times evidence weight, normalized:

| Branch | Prior | Evidence weight | Posterior | Move |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Incumbent Absorbs | 40.0% | 0.33493 | 56.8% | ×1.42 |
| Insurgent Wins Category | 30.0% | 0.23506 | 29.9% | ×1.00 |
| Substrate Commoditizes (null) | 30.0% | 0.10467 | 13.3% | ×0.44 |

Read this carefully. The ledger moved conviction toward Incumbent Absorbs and away from the null, while leaving Insurgent Wins almost exactly where the prior had it. That is itself informative: the evidence for the insurgent, S2 and S3, was real but offset by S1's strong, high-grade signal for the incumbent. A flat move ratio is not a non-result. It is the ledger reporting that the new evidence was a wash for that branch specifically.

### 4.4 The independence assumption, stated where it is used

The product form in Section 4.3 is valid only if the signals are independent given the branch. Section 4.2's redundancy rule is the practical correction when that assumption is violated by correlated sources. State this assumption explicitly in every File that runs the spine, so a reader can check the ledger against it rather than discovering the assumption only by reverse-engineering the arithmetic.

### 4.5 Bayes factors and how to read them

The ratio of two branches' evidence weights, independent of the prior, is the Bayes factor. It is the cleanest single number for stating how decisively the evidence, as opposed to the starting belief, favors one branch over another.

In the worked example: the Bayes factor of Incumbent Absorbs over the null is 0.33493 / 0.10467, or 3.2 to 1. The Bayes factor of Insurgent Wins over the null is 0.23506 / 0.10467, or 2.25 to 1.

Reporting Bayes factors alongside posteriors lets a reader separate "the evidence favors this" from "the prior already favored this and the evidence barely moved it," which matters whenever a File's prior carries a house view that needs checking against what the data alone would say. Read the magnitude against the Kass-Raftery scale rather than by feel:

| Bayes factor | Strength of evidence |
|---|---|
| 1 to 3 | Barely worth a mention |
| 3 to 20 | Positive |
| 20 to 150 | Strong |
| Above 150 | Decisive |

A 3.2 to 1 factor is therefore positive but not strong, a tilt rather than a verdict. A File should say exactly that rather than letting a three-to-one ratio read as a settled case.

### 4.6 The floor and the residual branch

Every File carries a floor, conventionally 5 percent, below which no branch is allowed to report a bare computed value. The floor exists because a branch computing to 1 or 2 percent usually reflects the limits of the analyst's likelihood judgments, not genuine near-impossibility, and reporting false precision at the tail invites overconfidence exactly where the evidence is thinnest.

The multi-branch procedure is a single simultaneous pass, not a sequential one. Identify every branch that falls below the floor. Raise each to the floor value at once. Compute the total amount added across all floored branches. Remove that same total, in proportion to each surviving branch's share of the remaining mass, from the branches that were above the floor. Renormalize once. Running this sequentially, one branch at a time, produces a different and path-dependent answer depending on the order chosen, which is the error this clarification closes.

### 4.7 The conditional causal tier, correctly scoped

The causal tier is conditional. It runs only when a realized outcome panel exists, meaning actual resolved outcomes across comparable past cases, not merely opinions about a single unresolved case. When the panel does not exist or is too small, the tier is explicitly gated off and held, not silently skipped. A File should say "held, insufficient panel" rather than leaving the section out, since the gap itself is informative.

The tier has four components in three categories, not four parallel estimator families as an earlier edition implied:

**Two causal estimators**, which estimate an actual causal effect:
- A causal forest, estimating a conditional average treatment effect, for instance the differential effect of a deal structure on time-to-scale across comparable cases.
- A Bayesian structural time series event study, estimating a counterfactual trajectory around a single dated intervention, for instance what an adoption curve would have done absent a specific regulatory action.

**One attribution component**, which is prediction plus explanation rather than causal estimation:
- A gradient-boosted classifier predicting which branch a new case resembles, paired with Shapley attributions that decompose the prediction into per-feature contributions. This tells you what features drove the prediction. It does not, by itself, tell you that those features caused the outcome.

**One regime-conditioning model**:
- A hidden Markov model inferring which latent market regime is currently active. Regime is latent, so condition on the full regime posterior, a distribution over regimes, rather than hard-assigning a single regime label. Hard-assignment discards exactly the uncertainty the model exists to carry.

When the panel is too small to run the causal forest or BSTS responsibly, conventionally fewer than roughly eight to ten comparable resolved cases, present the available cases as a structured comparison table instead, and use the attribution component qualitatively as a feature read rather than a trained model. Say so plainly. A small honest comparison is worth more than a model run past its statistical license.

### 4.8 The cascade as a structural causal model

Where Part Three Section 3.5 introduces the cascade narratively, the spine renders it as a structural causal model with named paths and, where data supports it, estimated path coefficients. Each node is a market state, each edge is a mechanism, and the model should be falsifiable in the same sense as the branch map: a stated counterfactual that, if observed, would force a redrawn cascade rather than a defended one.

### 4.9 The cross-cycle update, corrected

Within a cycle, Section 4.1 through 4.6 produce a posterior. Across cycles, that posterior has to become the next cycle's prior without breaking falsifiability. An earlier edition's rule added a multiple of the posterior itself to the pseudo-count vector every cycle. That rule has two faults stacked together. The total pseudo-count α₀ grows without bound, since nothing ever leaves it, so prior strength inflates indefinitely. And the rule re-adds the posterior, which already embeds the prior, rather than the new evidence alone, so old conviction compounds on top of itself. The combined effect is a File that drifts correctly in direction but moves by a shrinking amount each cycle, becoming asymptotically unfalsifiable, which directly contradicts the kill-condition discipline in Part Six.

**The corrected rule.** A forgetting factor λ on the existing pseudo-counts, plus evidence-derived counts added fresh each cycle:

α ← λ·α + m·ê

Here α is the pseudo-count vector entering the cycle, ê is this cycle's normalized evidence weight vector (the evidence-weight terms from Section 4.3, normalized to sum to one), m is the total count of new evidence-strength added this cycle, conventionally calibrated to the number and grade of new signals, and λ is the forgetting factor, conventionally in the 0.7 to 0.9 range depending on how fast a File's market actually moves.

This rule has a finite steady state. If m and λ are held constant, total pseudo-count strength converges to α₀ = m / (1 − λ), rather than growing forever. At λ = 0.8 and m = 5, for instance, the steady state is α₀ = 5 / 0.2 = 25, meaning the File's accumulated conviction asymptotes to the weight of 25 pseudo-observations and stays there, sticky but never immovable. A File that keeps receiving strong contrary evidence will keep moving, cycle after cycle, by a bounded and predictable amount rather than a shrinking one.

### 4.10 Calibration scoring and the sensitivity sweep

Two verification disciplines that an earlier edition omitted, both folded into the maintenance stage in Part Six.

**Calibration scoring.** A File that issues a posterior is issuing a probabilistic forecast, and forecasts can be scored against what actually happens. When a branch resolves, meaning the underlying event becomes a recorded fact rather than a live question, score that File's final pre-resolution posterior against the outcome using a proper scoring rule, a Brier score or a log score. Track this across the whole book of Files, not File by File in isolation. A practitioner whose 70 percent calls resolve true roughly 70 percent of the time across the book is calibrated. One whose 70 percent calls resolve true 90 percent of the time is sandbagging, stating less confidence than the evidence supports. One whose 70 percent calls resolve true 40 percent of the time has a systematic bias somewhere in the likelihood judgments, most often in how aggressively Grade C and projection signals get weighted.

**The sensitivity sweep.** Before publishing a reweight, perturb the analyst-supplied likelihoods in the ledger, conventionally by plus or minus ten to fifteen percent on each value, and recompute the posterior. If the leading branch survives every reasonable perturbation, the result is robust and the File can say so with confidence. If a plausible perturbation flips the leading branch, that fragility is itself the most important thing in the File that cycle, more important than the headline number, and should be reported as the central finding rather than buried in a footnote.


## Part Five: A complete run, start to finish

Carry the Section 4.3 example through the rest of the Machine to see how the parts connect.

**Stage one and two, surface and detect.** The trigger signal is S1, a realized revenue disclosure. Run the tells: convergence fires, since rivals are repositioning onto the same contested layer using the incumbent's language. Run the force scan: the binding force is rivalry between the incumbent and the insurgent over who owns the layer the revenue line just proved is monetizable. Name the constraint: economic, the question is whether the insurgent's unit economics can reach scale before the incumbent's distribution advantage closes the gap. Clock: cost-curve, predictable but contested.

**Stage three, moat test.** Run the six tests on the insurgent's moat. It passes learning compounding and economic conversion is now confirmed by S1's existence as a contested fact. It is weak on control point leverage, since the incumbent's distribution still gates most of the addressable market.

**Stage four, ownership map.** The contested layer sits between the incumbent, who owns distribution, and the insurgent, who owns the technical lead. Neither owns the buyer's underlying budget constraint outright.

**Stage five, owner's game.** The incumbent's rent route is toll, charging for access to its distribution rather than building the layer itself, which is exactly the kind of move that shows up as a convergence tell once competitors start copying the language.

**Stage six, the quantitative spine.** This is Section 4.3's worked update: Incumbent Absorbs at 56.8 percent, Insurgent Wins at 29.9 percent, the null at 13.3 percent, all above the 5 percent floor so no flooring pass is needed this cycle. Bayes factors of 3.2 to 1 and 2.25 to 1 over the null, both positive but not strong on Kass-Raftery, so the File reports a tilt rather than a settled case.

**Stage seven, the cascade.** If Incumbent Absorbs continues to lead, the next link in the cascade is the insurgent's remaining independent customers, who become the new contested layer between the incumbent and whoever serves customers the incumbent's model does not fit.

**Stage eight, translate to action.** The incumbent's move is to keep tolling rather than building, since toll is working and absorption is expensive. The insurgent's move is to find the segment the incumbent's distribution structurally cannot serve and defend it as a beachhead rather than fighting for the whole layer. A capital allocator's move is to wait for the next cycle's reweight before committing, since the current factor is positive but not strong.


## Part Six: Governance

### 6.1 The Unguarded Flank

Three gates, run against every candidate opening a File surfaces.

**Structural block.** Is there a specific reason no current player can occupy this position, not merely that none has yet?

**Constraint, not feature.** Does the position sit on the binding constraint itself, rather than on a feature that is nice to have but not gating?

**Compounds before arrival.** Does waiting let someone else's flywheel lap the position before a new entrant could reach it?

A candidate opening must pass all three to count as a real flank. Passing two and failing one is the most common analyst error, mistaking a real gap for a defensible one.

### 6.2 The Governor and two-speed control

The constraint thesis at the top of a File moves slowly and only on a named kill condition, never on routine evidence. The branch weights and the signal ledger move quickly, every cycle, as evidence arrives. Collapsing these two speeds into one, reweighting the thesis itself every time a signal lands, is the most common way a File loses its own discipline and starts chasing noise.

### 6.3 Generation-verification separation

A number is never trusted because it was computed. It is trusted because it was checked. Whoever runs the update should not be the sole check on whether the inputs were reasonable; a second pass, even a self-administered one done at a different time with fresh eyes, should re-examine the likelihood judgments before a reweight publishes.

### 6.4 The four-action maintenance classification

Every cycle, a File receives exactly one of four dispositions.

| Disposition | When it applies |
|---|---|
| Ledger note | New evidence arrived but did not move the posterior meaningfully |
| Reweight | New evidence moved the posterior enough to warrant a republished branch map |
| Thesis review | A named kill condition fired, requiring the constraint thesis itself, not just the weights, to be re-examined |
| Retirement | The constraint has fully resolved, or the thesis has been structurally falsified |

Calibration scoring and the sensitivity sweep from Section 4.10 are run at every reweight, before publication, and their results are logged alongside the disposition.


## Part Seven: File anatomy and writing discipline

### 7.1 Required sections, in working order

A complete File contains, at minimum: a constraint thesis with type and clock; a force scan ranked by the binding force; an ownership map with the owner's-game rent route named; the six moat tests run against the load-bearing moat; a weighted branch map summing to 100 percent with the floor applied as a single simultaneous pass; a dated signal ledger with grade, temper exponent, and per-branch likelihoods; the posterior, Bayes factors against the null, and the Kass-Raftery read; the conditional causal tier, run or explicitly held; the cascade, mapped at least two links forward; the Unguarded Flank, tested against all three gates; actor reads for the major classes of participant the File concerns, an incumbent, an insurgent or challenger, a capital allocator, and a regulator or buyer, at minimum; a maintenance log with the four-action disposition; the sensitivity sweep result; and source-graded references.

### 7.2 Source grading

| Grade | Definition | Typical τ |
|---|---|---|
| A | Motive-clean primary: filings, primary data, direct disclosure | 1.0 |
| B | Motive-aware or credible secondary: analyst notes, trade press, vendor-adjacent commentary | 0.5 to 0.6 |
| C | Secondary pending verification, or projection: founder interviews about future plans, unverified claims | 0.15 to 0.3 |

### 7.3 The eight argument structures

Every File and every essay built from it is led by one structure, with at most one supporting structure. Concede-then-pivot is the default in collaborative or co-creation contexts.

Concede-then-pivot. Thesis-and-kill-signal. Constraint-first. Steelman-then-break. Structural disqualification. Cost-of-error asymmetry. Pattern accumulation. Two-sided weighted brief.

### 7.4 Writing discipline

No em dashes. Paragraphs of three to five sentences, roughly sixty to one hundred twenty words, one idea per paragraph, with deliberate variation in sentence length. No anaphora stacking, no rhetorical question chains, no antithetical "not X but Y" constructions, no ordinal scaffolding in prose, no staccato single-line paragraphs, no bold run-in labels outside instrument sections such as tables. Uncertainty is carried in the weights, not in adjectives. The strongest opposing argument is conceded specifically, by name, before any pivot.


## Reviewer's changelog from the prior edition

This edition corrects one methodological bug, one framing error, and several smaller imprecisions, and adds two verification disciplines that were missing. All worked-example arithmetic was checked computationally and reproduces exactly; the numbers in Part Four are unchanged from the prior edition except where new explicit inputs were supplied to make an example reproducible.

The bug, in Section 4.9, was a cross-cycle update that inflated prior strength without bound and recycled the posterior into itself, making a long-lived File asymptotically unfalsifiable. It is replaced by the forgetting-factor-plus-evidence-counts rule, with a finite steady-state prior strength.

The framing error, in Section 4.1, described the belief layer as a Dirichlet-multinomial conjugate update and justified it by a conjugacy the within-cycle procedure does not actually preserve. It is now described correctly as a tempered, power-likelihood Bayesian update over a latent categorical hypothesis, with the Dirichlet supplying the prior and the cross-cycle conjugate carryover only.

Smaller corrections: the conditional-independence assumption behind the product-update rule is now stated explicitly, in Section 4.4; the causal tier, Section 4.7, is described as two causal estimators plus one attribution component plus one regime model, rather than four parallel estimator families, and the regime model now conditions on the full regime posterior rather than a hard-assigned label; the redundancy example in Section 4.2 now carries explicit likelihoods so it is reproducible; the Bayes-factor section adds the Kass-Raftery interpretive scale; the multi-branch floor procedure is clarified as a single simultaneous pass rather than a sequential one; and the force scan notes that the sixth force, complementors, is the value-net extension to Porter's original five.

Additions: Section 4.10 introduces calibration scoring, a proper scoring rule run against realized outcomes across the whole book of Files, and a per-cycle sensitivity sweep, perturbing analyst likelihoods to test whether the leading branch is robust. Both are folded into the maintenance discipline in Part Six and the required-sections list in Part Seven.

This is the canonical methodology and application playbook for the What If Next Machine. Worked examples in Parts Four and Five are illustrative and do not correspond to any single published File. Live File numbers and weights are computed fresh against that File's own evidence each cycle.
